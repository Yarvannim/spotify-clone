2. Information We Collect

We collect the following information to provide and improve our service to you:

A. Information You Provide Directly:

  • Account Data: When you create an account, we collect your email address, a username, a password, and your full name.
  • Profile Data: You may choose to set a display name for your profile.
  • Payment Data: We use Polar.sh as our payment processor. We do not store your full payment card details on our servers. Polar.sh handles all transaction data in accordance with their own privacy policy.

B. Information Collected Automatically:

  • Device and Connection Information: We collect your IP address for essential security and operational purposes, such as preventing abuse and diagnosing technical issues.
  • Usage Data: To provide a personalized experience and improve our service, we collect data about your interactions with the App. This includes your listening history, song skips, and other usage patterns.
  • Cookies and Similar Technologies: We use cookies strictly for authentication and essential session management to keep you logged in while using the App.

4. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your personal data. We only share it in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We engage trusted third-party companies to perform functions on our behalf:
    • Amazon Web Services (AWS): We use AWS, with servers located in Frankfurt, Germany, for hosting our application and databases.
    • Polar.sh: We use Polar.sh as our payment service provider.
    These providers are bound by contractual obligations to keep your information confidential and use it only for the purposes for which we disclose it to them.
  • Business Transfers: If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or a portion of our assets, your data may be transferred to the new entity as part of that transaction. We will notify you and ensure the transfer is conducted in a way that respects your privacy.
  • Legal Obligation: We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to a valid legal request, such as a court order or subpoena.

5. International Data Transfers

All your personal data is stored and processed on servers within the European Economic Area (EEA), specifically in the AWS EU (Frankfurt) region. We do not transfer your personal data outside the EEA.

6. Data Retention

We will retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy.

  • Account Data: We retain the data associated with your account for as long as your account is active. If your account is inactive for a period of 2 years, we will proceed to delete your data.
  • Transaction Data: We are legally required to retain financial transaction data for 7 years for tax and accounting purposes.
  • Upon deletion of your account, we will remove your personal data from our active databases, though some anonymized or aggregated data may be retained for analytics.

7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. These measures include encryption, access controls, and secure server infrastructure provided by AWS.

8. Your Data Protection Rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights, which you can exercise through the "Privacy Settings" in your user profile or by contacting us at the email address provided above:

  • Right of Access: You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You can update or correct any inaccurate personal data in your profile.
  • Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): You can request the deletion of your account and personal data.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You can limit the way we use your data (e.g., by turning off certain data processing features in your privacy settings). Please note this may limit the functionality of the App.
  • Right to Data Portability: You can request to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format (e.g., JSON).
  • Right to Object: You can object to certain types of processing, such as for marketing purposes.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Where we rely on your consent (if any), you can withdraw it at any time in your privacy settings, without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

If you wish to lodge a complaint about our handling of your data, you have the right to contact your local data protection supervisory authority.

9. Children's Data

Our App is not directed at children under the age at which parental consent is required in their country of residence (e.g., 16 in some EEA countries). We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe we have collected data from a child, please contact us immediately.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page will indicate when changes were made. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of the App after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the new policy.
